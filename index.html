Is it true that drinking alcohol and happiness levels are linked in every country in the world?

Do you know that happiness can be measured and each country is ranked each year based on its happiness index? On a scale of 0 to 10, this index evaluates the level of happiness in various nations. Some people believe that alcohol consumption can increase happiness. So is it true?

World Happiness Rate in 2016

Every year, the World Happiness Index polls a large number of people from various countries around the world to determine which country has the happiest people. Denmark is this year's winner.

Top 10 Happiness Country with their Alcohol Consumption

Surprisingly, Denmark, which is the happiest country, has a high consumption rate of about 583 liters per person.

Does the happiness score in every country come with alcohol consumption?

To conclude, the happiness score in several countries, especially in Western Europe, is directly proportional to the amount of alcohol consumed.

Does the happiness score in every country come with a Human Development Index?

To conclude, the happiness score is linearly proportional to the human development index, with the highest position in the scattered plot being Norway.
